HOW THE·CORPS·WORKS

Four rules. Three pillars. Wage first. Members own it.

The CCC Boys is a worker-owned cooperative under South Carolina Chapter 45. Members run it. Members vote it. Members get paid first. The corps takes care of its own. Everyone else who supports the work is honored too, on terms that fit them.

THE FOUR RULES

RULE I
THE GOLDEN RULE
Treat people the way we would want to be treated. The whole foundation.
RULE II
DON'T EXPLOIT
Revenue flows toward people, not from their desperation. If the model needs someone's worst day, it is the wrong model.
RULE III
BE KIND
Build things that are actually helpful. Tone matters. People remember how you treated them when they needed something.
RULE IV
BE THOUGHTFUL
Slow on hard-to-reverse decisions. Fast on reversible ones. Prefer the honest answer.

THE THREE PILLARS

The corps stands on three legs. Each pillar earns its own way. All three share the same handshake.

⚓
TRADES
Marine work out of Walterboro, hurricane prep along the coast, shingle recycling depot, apprenticeships that pay a livable wage on day one.
⌨
SOFTWARE
A library of $1 trade apps. NEC code lookup, conduit fill, panel schedule, marine electrical, OSHA checklists. Members and Apprentices get every app free.
⚖
RAILS
A neobank built on Dakota with per-product accounts, passkey-signed votes, and wage-first ordering encoded in code. No VC. No outside equity. Ever.

WHO BELONGS TO THE CORPS

Five tiers. Two roads. The road from outside to inside runs through Apprentice. The road of support runs alongside.

FOUNDER
Original cofounders. A founder is a Member first, with additional terms layered on top.
INSIDE
MEMBER
Voting owner. Paid wage-first. Receives surplus. Free access to every app.
INSIDE
APPRENTICE
Training on the trades or the software side. Paid an apprentice wage on day one. Path to Member.
INSIDE
PATRON
Optional $1 per month dues. Unlocks every current and future app. Name on the supporter roster.
OUTSIDE
SUPPORTER
Pays $1 once to unlock a specific app at cccboys.com. Has an account on the corps roster.
OUTSIDE

See the full tier explanations for who is in each, what they pay, what they get, what they owe.

WHERE THE MONEY GOES

Every product (an app, the depot, the marine pillar) has its own account on CCC Boys rails. Money flows in. It flows out in this exact order:

1
WAGES
Members and Apprentices who worked on this product draw their wage first. Always. Before anything else.
↓
2
OPERATING COSTS
Servers, materials, distribution, the costs that keep the product alive.
↓
3
RUNWAY RESERVE
A year of runway in the account before any surplus moves. Five years over time.
↓
4
CORPS CROSS-SUBSIDY
A share to the general fund. Apprentice wages on slower products. Capital reserves. Grants matching.
↓
5
SURPLUS TO MEMBERS
Whatever is left goes back to the people who built it, proportional to contribution. Not shareholders. Not investors. The builders.

See the neobank page for the technical detail.

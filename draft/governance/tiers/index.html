LEVELS OF·BELONGING

Five tiers. Two roads. The handshake is the same on every rung.
Governance › Tiers
NAMING RESOLVED
The earlier draft proposed a recurring Patron tier at $1 a month. That tier is retired. Two recurring tiers at the door was one too many. The outside-the-corps membership is now a single Virtual Member at $100 a year, which unlocks the whole cheap-tier library and comes with a physical card and a member number. One-off buyers are simply Customers: a dollar for a one-shot app, or a dollar a month for a living service. No badge, no number, just a fair transaction.
INSIDE THE CORPS
TIER 01 · THE COFOUNDERS
CORE MEMBER
Corps Member first. Core on top.

The cofounders of the corps (Justin Shearer, Joe Nettles, Jeremy Shearer, Jabbey, plus any future founder additions by unanimous existing-founder vote). A Core Member is a Corps Member with additional layered terms: vesting on early capital contributions, board-equivalent seats on cross-pillar decisions, deeper voting weight on existential matters like bylaws changes and dissolution.

HOW YOU JOIN
Cofounded the corps at formation, or added later by unanimous existing-Founder vote. Cannot be voted out by membership majority. Can resign.
WHAT YOU PAY
Capital contribution at formation (cash, equipment, or labor). Ongoing: same wage-first rules as any Member.
WHAT YOU GET
All Corps Member rights, plus board-equivalent voting weight on existential decisions, plus a founder-specific surplus track per the formation agreement.
WHAT YOU OWE
Active participation in corps operations. Stewardship of the brand and the four rules. Long-term commitment to the cooperative structure.
Note: Core Member status does not exempt anyone from Corps Member rules. The wage-first ordering, the four rules, and the cooperative voting structure apply to Core Members too.
TIER 02 · THE VOTING OWNERS
CORPS MEMBER
You voted in. You own a share of the work.

Corps Members are the voting owners of the corps. They draw wages first, share in surplus, vote on decisions, and get free access to every CCC Boys app as part of belonging. Membership is earned through participation, not bought.

HOW YOU JOIN
Graduate from Apprenticeship (12 to 24 months typical), or direct admit by Member supermajority. Either path requires a Member sponsor.
WHAT YOU PAY
Your labor at the corps standard hourly rate, on whatever pillar suits your skills. Optional: a small one-time membership stake to formalize the position.
WHAT YOU GET
Wage first ordering on every product you work on. Surplus distribution. One vote in member matters. Free access to every CCC Boys app. Your name on the roster.
WHAT YOU OWE
The four rules. Honest hours. Showing up for the work. Participation in member votes. Mentorship of at least one Apprentice when capacity allows.
TIER 03 · THE TRAINEES
APPRENTICE
Learning the trade. Paid like it matters.

Apprentices are in training on either the trades side (marine, roofing, hurricane prep, depot operations) or the software side (apps, infrastructure, AI tooling). The apprentice wage is set at a livable hourly rate on day one, not a stipend. Apprenticeship typically runs 12 to 24 months.

HOW YOU JOIN
Apply through cccboys.com. Interview with a Member sponsor. Accepted by a simple Member majority. No requirement of prior trade or coding experience.
WHAT YOU PAY
Your labor at the apprentice hourly rate. Time and attention. Willingness to be taught.
WHAT YOU GET
Livable apprentice wage from day one. Hands-on training. A Member as mentor. Free access to every CCC Boys app. A real path to Member status.
WHAT YOU OWE
Show up. Learn the four rules. Treat tools and people well. Complete the apprenticeship curriculum (trades or software). Pass the journey check at the end.
Two-track Apprenticeship: the trades side and the software side run in parallel. A trades apprentice might work the depot in the morning and study for the electrical license in the afternoon. A software apprentice might ship app features one week and pair-program with Jabbey on the neobank rails the next. Apprentices can cross tracks.
OUTSIDE THE CORPS
TIER 04 · THE SUPPORTING MEMBERSHIP
VIRTUAL MEMBER
A hundred a year. The whole library. A card with your number.

Virtual Members pay $100 a year. That unlocks every $1 one-shot app and every $1/month service in the catalog, the whole cheap tier, current and future. They receive a physical membership card with a unique member number, a profile in the public directory (opt-in), and a direct channel to give product feedback. A Virtual Member is not a Corps Member: they do not draw wages or share in surplus. The "Virtual" keeps the word Member honest for the people doing the labor. Premium products stay separately priced (a member discount is possible, but that is lawyer territory).

HOW YOU JOIN
Create a free account on cccboys.com. Pay the $100 a year. Your card and member number are issued. Renews annually; lapse and the unlock pauses until you renew.
WHAT YOU PAY
$100 per year. Paid on corps rails (Stripe today, Dakota neobank as it comes online), not Apple or Google.
WHAT YOU GET
Every $1 one-shot app and $1/month service unlocked. A physical card with your member number. A directory profile (opt-in). A feedback channel into the product roadmap.
WHAT YOU OWE
Nothing beyond the dues. You are not asked to vote, work, or evangelize. We will not spam you. The corps takes the support, ships the work, says thank you.
TIER 05 · PAY PER USE
CUSTOMER
A dollar an app, or a dollar a month. No card, no number.

Customers pay per use: $1 once to unlock a one-shot app (lifetime access to that app on that account), or $1 a month for a living service with ongoing compute on our side. No subscription lock-in beyond the service you chose, no upsell ads inside the apps, no tier badge or member number. Anyone scrolling a category in the App Store and frustrated with the noise can find the CCC Boys version, pay a dollar, and get on with their day.

HOW YOU JOIN
No account required to buy. Browse the app library, pay $1 to unlock a one-shot app or start a $1/month service, sign in on your phone. Done.
WHAT YOU PAY
$1 once per one-shot app, or $1 per month per service. Paid on corps rails. No App Store cut. If the corps ever decommissions an app, your unlock is honored anyway.
WHAT YOU GET
Access to exactly what you paid for: lifetime on a one-shot unlock, or ongoing while a service subscription is active. An honest tool with no noise.
WHAT YOU OWE
Nothing. Use the app. Tell the next tradesperson in your shop if you liked it. That is the distribution model.

LEVELS OF·BELONGING

Five tiers. Two roads. The handshake is the same on every rung.
Governance › Tiers
PROPOSED NAMING
We need a name for the recurring tier (the $1 a month one). Subscriber reads like SaaS. Recurring Supporter is honest but bland. The working draft proposes Patron, the historical name for someone who funds work they believe in. Other candidates to weigh: Sustainer (used by NPR, has nonprofit dignity), Standing Supporter (descriptive but flat), Friend of the Corps (warm but soft). Justin's call. The drafts below use Patron throughout.
INSIDE THE CORPS
TIER 01 · THE COFOUNDERS
FOUNDER
Member first. Founder on top.

The cofounders of the corps (Justin Connolley, Joey Nettles, Jeremy Connolley, Jabbey, plus any future founder additions by unanimous existing-founder vote). A Founder is a Member with additional layered terms: vesting on early capital contributions, board-equivalent seats on cross-pillar decisions, deeper voting weight on existential matters like bylaws changes and dissolution.

HOW YOU JOIN
Cofounded the corps at formation, or added later by unanimous existing-Founder vote. Cannot be voted out by membership majority. Can resign.
WHAT YOU PAY
Capital contribution at formation (cash, equipment, or labor). Ongoing: same wage-first rules as any Member.
WHAT YOU GET
All Member rights, plus board-equivalent voting weight on existential decisions, plus a founder-specific surplus track per the formation agreement.
WHAT YOU OWE
Active participation in corps operations. Stewardship of the brand and the four rules. Long-term commitment to the cooperative structure.
Note: Founder status does not exempt anyone from member-tier rules. The wage-first ordering, the four rules, and the cooperative voting structure apply to Founders too.
TIER 02 · THE VOTING OWNERS
MEMBER
You voted in. You own a share of the work.

Members are the voting owners of the corps. They draw wages first, share in surplus, vote on decisions, and get free access to every CCC Boys app as part of belonging. Membership is earned through participation, not bought.

HOW YOU JOIN
Graduate from Apprenticeship (12 to 24 months typical), or direct admit by Member supermajority. Either path requires a Member sponsor.
WHAT YOU PAY
Your labor at the corps standard hourly rate, on whatever pillar suits your skills. Optional: a small one-time membership stake to formalize the position.
WHAT YOU GET
Wage first ordering on every product you work on. Surplus distribution. One vote in member matters. Free access to every CCC Boys app. Your name on the roster.
WHAT YOU OWE
The four rules. Honest hours. Showing up for the work. Participation in member votes. Mentorship of at least one Apprentice when capacity allows.
TIER 03 · THE TRAINEES
APPRENTICE
Learning the trade. Paid like it matters.

Apprentices are in training on either the trades side (marine, roofing, hurricane prep, depot operations) or the software side (apps, infrastructure, AI tooling). The apprentice wage is set at a livable hourly rate on day one, not a stipend. Apprenticeship typically runs 12 to 24 months.

HOW YOU JOIN
Apply through cccboys.com. Interview with a Member sponsor. Accepted by a simple Member majority. No requirement of prior trade or coding experience.
WHAT YOU PAY
Your labor at the apprentice hourly rate. Time and attention. Willingness to be taught.
WHAT YOU GET
Livable apprentice wage from day one. Hands-on training. A Member as mentor. Free access to every CCC Boys app. A real path to Member status.
WHAT YOU OWE
Show up. Learn the four rules. Treat tools and people well. Complete the apprenticeship curriculum (trades or software). Pass the journey check at the end.
Two-track Apprenticeship: the trades side and the software side run in parallel. A trades apprentice might work the depot in the morning and study for the electrical license in the afternoon. A software apprentice might ship app features one week and pair-program with Jabbey on the neobank rails the next. Apprentices can cross tracks.
OUTSIDE THE CORPS
TIER 04 · RECURRING SUPPORTERS
PATRON
A dollar a month. The whole library, always.

Patrons pay an optional $1 per month in supporter dues. In exchange they get every current and every future CCC Boys app unlocked, the supporter badge, their name on the public roster, and the satisfaction of being one of the people who keeps the work going. Not a customer, a supporter.

HOW YOU JOIN
Create a free account on cccboys.com. Opt in to the $1 a month dues. Cancel any time. Comes back next month if you reactivate.
WHAT YOU PAY
$1 per month. Paid on corps rails (Stripe today, Dakota neobank as it comes online), not Apple or Google.
WHAT YOU GET
Every current and future CCC Boys app unlocked. Patron badge on your account. Name on the supporter roster (opt-in). Honest gratitude.
WHAT YOU OWE
Nothing beyond the dues. You are not asked to vote, work, or evangelize. We will not spam you. The corps takes the support, ships the work, says thank you.
TIER 05 · ONE-TIME UNLOCKS
SUPPORTER
A dollar an app. As many as you need.

Supporters pay $1 once for each app they want unlocked. Lifetime access to that specific app for that account. No subscription. No upsell ads inside the apps. Anyone scrolling a category in the App Store and frustrated with the noise can find the CCC Boys version, pay a dollar, and get on with their day.

HOW YOU JOIN
Create a free account on cccboys.com. Browse the app library. Pay $1 to unlock the apps you want. Sign in to the app on your phone. Done.
WHAT YOU PAY
$1 once per app. Paid on corps rails. No App Store cut. If the corps ever decommissions an app, your unlock is honored anyway.
WHAT YOU GET
Lifetime access to the specific app you unlocked. Supporter badge on your account. The right to say you supported the corps.
WHAT YOU OWE
Nothing. Use the app. Tell the next tradesperson in your shop if you liked it. That is the distribution model.

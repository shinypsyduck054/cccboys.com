How the math works

The model rolls all revenue into a single pool, subtracts operating costs and wages (wage-first ordering), and treats what's left as the corps surplus pool. Your wage is your hours times your tier rate. Your surplus share is whatever percentage you set.

total_revenue = app_sales + sub_revenue + premium_revenue + virtual_member_revenue + recycling + hurricane_prep + marine
app_sales = $1 apps_count × avg_paid_uses_per_app
sub_revenue = $1/mo services_count × avg_subscribers × 12 months
premium_revenue = premium_count × avg_revenue_per_premium
virtual_member_revenue = vm_count × $100
total_wages = (apprentices × apprentice_wage + corps_members × member_wage) × hours_per_week × 50 weeks
operating_costs = total_revenue × operating_pct
corps_surplus = max(0, total_revenue − total_wages − operating_costs)
your_wage = your_hours × corps_member_wage
your_surplus_share = corps_surplus × your_share_pct
your_total = your_wage + your_surplus_share

Active weeks per year defaulted to 50 (2 weeks paid time off). Wage is computed at the Corps Member rate for the personal slice; if modeling an apprentice, multiply manually or use a separate scenario.

What the presets do

Year 1 (lean): 2 apprentices, 3 Corps Members (Justin FT, Jeremy 40 hr, Joey occasional). Small catalog. Modest trade pillar revenue. Recycling is mostly a dump collecting tipping fees.

Year 3 (growing): 10 apprentices, 20 Corps Members across SC. Catalog at 200 apps. Recycling has equipment. Hurricane prep team established.

Year 5+ (at scale): 100 apprentices, 200 Corps Members across multiple state chapters. 2,000-app catalog. Multi-stream recycling. Real revenue across all pillars.

Bull case: Federation across 20 states. Thousands of apps. Tens of thousands of Virtual Members. A few breakout premium products. The wild scenario worth testing the math against.

PORTUGAL

A COOPERATIVE TRADITION. AND WHAT WE'RE DOING NOW.

Portugal has worker-owned cooperatives older than most of the people running it. The Carnation Revolution in 1974 opened the door to a wave of agricultural, industrial, and service cooperatives that are still operating. The legal framework (Código Cooperativo) is well-worn. The handshake is familiar.

THE COOPERATIVE TRADITION IN PORTUGAL

1974

THE COOPERATIVE EXPLOSION

After the Carnation Revolution, Portuguese workers and farmers began organizing into cooperatives at a pace the country had never seen. Agricultural cooperatives took over land in the Alentejo. Worker-owned manufacturing collectives formed across the industrial north. Fishing cooperatives organized along the entire Atlantic coast.

CONFAGRI, the federation of agricultural cooperatives, still represents more than seven hundred member cooperatives. CASES (Cooperativa António Sérgio para a Economia Social) is the modern hub for the social economy across all sectors. The cooperative is not a foreign concept in Portugal. It is a standing institution.

What we are doing now (worker-owned, surplus to the people who made the work, no shareholders) is what Portuguese cooperatives have been doing for fifty years. The form translates without an accent.

2026

WHAT THE WORK COULD LOOK LIKE HERE

Portugal has its own version of the work the CCC Boys are doing in South Carolina. Coastal storm prep along the Algarve and the Atlantic coast. Forest fire mitigation in the interior, where the summer fires get worse every year. Marine apprenticeships in the historic fishing ports. Recycling and circular-economy work across the urban corridors.

And the software work that doesn't care where you live. Kits, small tools, things that earn their keep. AI as the partner, not the replacement.

Friends of the corps in Portugal know their communities better than we do. The framework, the technology, the apprenticeship curriculum, and the playbook are open source under Creative Commons. You take what fits.

THE CORPS IN 2026

A LOCAL CORPS, A NATIONAL MOVEMENT.

The CCC Boys corps is based in Colleton County, South Carolina. Three cousins, Justin, Jeremy, and Joey. The framework, the technology, the apprenticeship curriculum, and the playbook are all open source. As builders in their own communities raise their hands, branches form.

South Carolina is the first chapter. Portugal could be next. No fees. No buy-in. You are a friend of the corps, not a franchisee. The handshake stays the handshake.

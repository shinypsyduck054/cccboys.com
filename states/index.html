WHERE THEY WORKED, 1933 TO 1942. WHERE YOU ARE NOW.

The Civilian Conservation Corps put up roughly forty-five hundred camps across all forty-eight contiguous states, Alaska, and Hawaii. Three million enrollees planted three billion trees, built eight hundred state parks, dug fire roads through wilderness that nobody could reach, and stitched soil back onto Dust Bowl farms. The work is still standing.

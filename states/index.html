EVERY·STATE

WHERE THEY WORKED, 1933 TO 1942. WHERE YOU ARE NOW.

The Civilian Conservation Corps put up roughly forty-five hundred camps across the lower forty-eight, plus the territories of Alaska and Hawaii. Three million enrollees planted three billion trees, built eight hundred state parks, dug fire roads through wilderness nobody else would reach, and stitched soil back onto Dust Bowl farms. The work is still standing.

YOUR STATE COULD BE NEXT.

The CCC Boys corps is based in Colleton County, South Carolina. The framework, the technology, the apprenticeship curriculum, and the playbook are all open source. As builders in their own communities raise their hands, branches form. South Carolina is the first. Yours could be next.

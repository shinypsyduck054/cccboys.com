WHAT THEY BUILT IN MICHIGAN

THE WORK IS STILL STANDING.

Michigan had one of the largest CCC programs in the country, particularly in the Upper Peninsula. Enrollees built Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park (the largest state park in Michigan), Tahquamenon Falls infrastructure, Ludington State Park, and Hartwick Pines. CCC crews planted hundreds of millions of trees across the cut-over Michigan pinelands, fought forest fires across the U.P., and built the lookout towers, bridges, and bathhouses that defined the Michigan state park system.

THE CORPS IN 2026

A COLLECTIVE OF BUILDERS.

The CCC Boys corps is based in Colleton County, South Carolina — three cousins, Justin, Jeremy, and Joey, building it from home. We do hurricane prep along the coast, shingle recycling out of the Columbia metro, marine apprenticeships in Walterboro, and a software portfolio with AI as the partner. The corps is worker-owned: members draw a steady wage first, then operating costs, then runway, then surplus to the people who made it happen. Not shareholders.

South Carolina is the first chapter. Other states are next, as builders in those places raise their hands. The playbook, curriculum, kit specs, and operating model are all open source under Creative Commons. Anyone can use them. Federated branches share the central infrastructure (a stablecoin-native neobank, a shared apprentice curriculum, a brand built by everyone) but operate locally. No fees. No buy-in. You are a branch, not a franchise.
